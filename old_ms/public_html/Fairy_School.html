MAGIC HILL
Summer Fairy School
2008

Magic Hill Summer Fairy School and Arts Workshop
At the Olalla home of Magical Strings
August 12, 13, 14   Tues – Thurs
10AM - 2PM

Philip and Pam Boulding of Magical Strings and Ann-Marie Hahn, Waldorf teacher for over 20 years, will weave days of storytelling, painting, singing, lyre playing, drawing from nature, knitting, fairy house and furniture making and adventures on Magic Hill, a beautiful fairyland in Olalla, just a ferry ride away from Seattle.


Magic Hill is Magical Strings’ 5 acre home overlooking Colvos passage and Vashon Island, located on the Kitsap Peninsula in Olalla, Washington. Through rolling meadows and ancient firs atop a hill, beneath maples and alders, there are fern pathways where each child will discover just the right spot to build a fairy home. There is a spacious light-filled classroom above Magical Strings’ studio where we will paint, do handwork, play the lyre, and other activities.

Come be a part of the Magic Hill experience!

 Ann-Marie Hahn, mother of two grown sons, has served children as a Waldorf grades and kindergarten teacher for the past 20 years in both California and Washington. Ann-Marie, grade two teacher at the Madrona School on Bainbridge Island, is a native of Sweden and brings a gift for storytelling and handwork with a special connection to nature spirits.

Pam Boulding, lyre teacher at the Bright Water, and the Seattle Waldorf School has been teaching music and painting to children in Waldorf schools throughout the Northwest for over twenty years. She holds a BA from Pitzer College, and for over 20 years has participated in Waldorf training courses including 10 years of eurythmy. Together with her husband Philip, the Bouldings and their five children form the musical ensemble Magical Strings, who perform their annual Celtic Yuletide concert throughout the Northwest. They have toured internationally and recorded 16 CD’s blending the sounds of Celtic harps, hammered dulcimers, violins, cello and other instruments from around the world.

Philip Boulding, musician, composer, teacher and harp maker since 1974, has inspired students of the Celtic harp worldwide with Magical Strings’ performances and in the Northwest through the School of Magical Strings and Magic Hill Summer Harp Camp. He has taught music and woodworking classes in Waldorf Schools and harp classes for children.  The Bouldings also design and manufacture instruments for an international clientele, including the lyres that they custom build for Waldorf Schools.
